# Design: AI in RAN — Signal vs. Hype Guide

## Problem Statement

A RAN engineer inside the industry wants to build a clear personal
understanding of where AI is actually working in RAN today, separate from
vendor claims and conference hype. The end goal: a structured, shareable
framework that maps RAN AI use cases by real maturity — and eventually
informs business case evaluation.

The problem isn't lack of information. There are thousands of vendor decks,
research papers, and conference talks. The problem is that no one has done
the honest separation: "this is deployed and producing measurable results"
vs. "this is a 2026 demo that won't be in production until 2028."

## Target Audience

Primary: yourself. Someone with RAN domain expertise who wants a trustworthy
personal reference, built from evidence rather than vendor marketing.

Secondary (when ready to share): team members, colleagues evaluating AI
investments, anyone asking "where should we actually focus in RAN AI?"

## Premises

1. The guide's value is not listing use cases. Vendors do that for free.
   The value is separating what's real today from what's 2+ years away.

2. In RAN, energy optimization has the fastest and most replicable ROI right
   now (weeks to deploy, 7-30% savings, multiple operator references). Beam
   management and predictive fault detection also show real results but require
   longer deployment cycles and more vendor-specific tuning.

3. O-RAN + xApps is where everyone is building toward, but only ~13% of
   operators are in production. The gap between ambition and reality is
   the most important thing to understand.

4. This needs to be a living document — the space moves fast enough that
   anything from 2025 is partially wrong today.

## Landscape Snapshot (April 2026)

### What's actually deployed and working

| Use case | Evidence | Typical result | Time to deploy |
|----------|----------|----------------|----------------|
| RAN energy optimization (sleep modes, carrier shutdown) | Vodafone 14% savings/site (Ericsson case study, 2023-2024); industry range 7-30% (RCR Wireless, Oct 2024) | 7-30% energy reduction | Weeks |
| Beam management / intelligent cell shaping | Ericsson AI-powered RAN blog (Jan 2023); Nokia Energy Efficiency deployments | 5% DL ↑, 30% UL ↑ throughput | Months |
| Predictive fault detection (NOC/SOC) | NVIDIA State of AI in Telco survey 2026; broad operator adoption cited | Reduced MTTR, fewer truck rolls | Months |
| Capacity planning and traffic prediction | Widely deployed in vendor planning tools (Nokia MIKA, Ericsson OSS) | Better capex allocation | Quarters |

### What's in trials / early production

| Use case | Current state | Realistic production horizon |
|----------|--------------|------------------------------|
| O-RAN xApps (real-time optimization) | ~13% operators in production O-RAN | 2027-2028 mainstream |
| AI-native RAN at edge (cell-site AI inference) | MWC 2026 demos, stadium pilots | 2027-2028 at scale |
| Closed-loop autonomous network functions | Requires human oversight still | 2027+ for Level 3+ autonomy |
| Interference management via AI | Active research, few production deployments | 2026-2027 |

### What's hype (not ready)

| Claim | Why it's not ready |
|-------|--------------------|
| Fully autonomous RAN (no human oversight) | Data quality, liability, regulatory constraints |
| "Plug-and-play" O-RAN subsystems | Multi-vendor integration remains complex |
| GenAI for real-time network management | Latency and reliability constraints |
| 6G AI-native architecture | Research/standards phase, deployment 2029+ |

## Approach: Structured Research Guide + Decision Framework

**Output:** A 10-15 page markdown guide structured around three questions:
1. What's working in RAN AI right now, and what's the evidence?
2. What's the ROI framework for evaluating a new use case?
3. What are the 5 failure modes and how do you avoid them?

**Format:** Markdown in `ai-telecom/` folder. One file per major section.
Version-controlled so you can see how your thinking evolves.

**Structure:**

```
ai-telecom/
├── design.md              (this file — project framing)
├── README.md              (index and how to use the guide)
├── 01-landscape.md        (maturity map — what's real vs. hype)
├── 02-use-cases/
│   ├── energy-optimization.md
│   ├── beam-management.md
│   ├── predictive-fault.md
│   └── o-ran-xapps.md
├── 03-roi-framework.md    (how to evaluate any new use case)
├── 04-failure-modes.md    (why most projects fail, with RAN specifics)
├── 05-business-cases.md   (potential opportunities — fill in over time)
└── sources.md             (all references with quality ratings)
```


## Success Criteria

- After 4 weeks of research: you can explain the maturity map to a colleague
  in 5 minutes without referencing the doc
- After 8 weeks: the ROI framework is detailed enough that you could apply
  it to a real use case your org is considering
- After 3 months: at least one section of the guide has been shared with
  someone who found it useful
- Stretch: the 05-business-cases.md section identifies 1-2 gaps that could
  be a product or service opportunity

## Open Questions
