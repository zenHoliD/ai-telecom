# NVIDIA State of AI in Telecom 2026 Survey

**Source:** NVIDIA Blog / Survey Report (2026)
**URL:** https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/ai-in-telco-survey-2026/
**Full report:** https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/lp/industries/telecommunications/state-of-ai-in-telecom-survey-report/
**Quality tier:** [IND] Independent survey (vendor-sponsored — apply appropriate skepticism)
**Relevant to:** `02-use-cases/predictive-fault.md`, `03-roi-framework.md`

---

## Survey scope

- Fourth annual survey
- 1,000+ telecom professionals worldwide
- Covers adoption, investment, ROI, use cases

---

## Key statistics

### Investment & adoption
- **89%** of telcos plan to increase AI budgets in 2026 (up from 65% in 2025)
- **35%** expect budget increases exceeding 10%
- **60%** using or assessing generative AI (up from 49% in 2024)
- **89%** consider open source models critical to their AI strategy

### ROI and impact
- **90%** report AI is increasing annual revenue and reducing costs
- **99%** say AI improved employee productivity
- **26%** cite major or significant improvement in task completion speed

### Top use cases for ROI
1. Autonomous networks — 50%
2. Improved customer service — 41%
3. Internal process optimization — 33%

**Fastest impact areas:** energy management, fault prediction, configuration drift correction, capacity planning

### Network autonomy
- **88%** of organizations operate at TM Forum autonomy levels 1–3
- **77%** expect AI-native networks to launch before full 6G deployment
- **65%** report network automation already driven by AI

---

## What this tells you

- Fault prediction and energy management are the two use cases with the widest confirmed ROI — validated by 1,000+ operators
- Autonomous networks (O-RAN xApps / closed-loop) is the #1 ROI use case by response, but 88% are still at L1–L3 autonomy — the gap between ambition and deployment is real
- GenAI adoption is accelerating but primarily in customer-facing and back-office roles, not network management

---

## Skepticism note

NVIDIA sells AI infrastructure to telcos. Framing "89% plan to increase AI spending" as a finding rather than a target is vendor interest aligned with survey incentives. Cross-reference adoption claims with operator earnings calls and independent analysts (Analysys Mason, Heavy Reading).
